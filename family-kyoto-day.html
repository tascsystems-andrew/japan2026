Kyoto With the Kids

The Plan

Three Stops, One Incredible Day

This is the whole crew's first taste of Kyoto — the ancient capital, 30 minutes from Osaka by train. We're hitting three of the city's most iconic spots, all chosen because they're genuinely amazing with young kids. Dan & Connie will stay overnight in Kyoto for their own self-guided day tomorrow.

Getting there: JR Special Rapid from Osaka Station to Kyoto Station (~30 min, covered by IC card, no reservation needed). From Kyoto Station, we'll use buses and taxis to get around the city. With 4 kids + 4 adults, a taxi between stops is often the same price as 8 bus fares and way less stressful.
9:30

Kiyomizu-dera

Higashiyama · ¥400 adults, free under 6, ¥200 ages 6–15 · 6:00–18:00

One of Japan's most iconic temples — a massive wooden terrace that juts out 13 metres over the hillside, supported by 139 pillars and not a single nail. The view over Kyoto from the terrace is breathtaking, especially in early November when the maples are turning.

At the base of the main hall, the Otowa Waterfall splits into three streams. Visitors line up to drink from one — each stream grants a different wish: success, love, or longevity. The kids will love choosing.

🧒 Kids love: The waterfall wishing game, the massive wooden terrace (feels like a treehouse), and the walk down through Higashiyama lanes with mochi shops, pottery stalls, and soft serve.

Tip: Walk down via Sannen-zaka and Ninnen-zaka — the prettiest lanes in Kyoto. Stone-paved, lantern-lined, full of snack shops. Stroller-possible but a carrier is easier.

11:30

Gion District + Lunch

Higashiyama · Free to stroll

Kyoto's famous geisha district. Walk along Hanamikoji-dori — the main street lined with traditional wooden machiya houses, tea houses, and restaurants. If you're lucky you might spot a maiko (apprentice geisha) heading to an appointment in full kimono.

Grab lunch here — plenty of options from casual udon shops to sit-down spots. The kids will be ready to refuel after the Kiyomizu walk.

🧒 Kids love: Spotting people in rental kimonos (they're everywhere), the little bridges over the canal on Shirakawa-dori, and the vending machine drinks.

Tip: For a quick, kid-friendly lunch, look for a kaiten-zushi (conveyor belt sushi) spot — the moving plates keep everyone entertained.

13:30

Kyoto Railway Museum

Shimogyo · ¥1,500 adults, ¥500 ages 3–12, free under 3 · 10:00–17:00 (last entry 16:30)

One of Japan's best train museums — and that's saying something in a country that takes trains very seriously. Highlights include a roundhouse full of real steam locomotives, a massive operating model railway diorama, a Shinkansen 500 series nose you can sit in, and hands-on driving simulators.

The outdoor area has a real steam locomotive that runs short rides (small extra fee). There's also a kids' play area and a café overlooking the tracks where you can watch real trains roll by. With the whole afternoon here, the kids can take their time with everything — no need to rush.

🧒 Kids love: Everything. The driving simulators, climbing into real train cabs, the steam train ride, and the diorama show (runs every 15 min). This is the kind of place where you have to drag them out at closing time.

Tip: The diorama show runs every 15 minutes and is worth seeing at least once — it's a massive model railway covering the entire Tokaido Shinkansen line. The steam train runs on a set schedule, so check the board when you arrive and plan around it.

D&C Evening Option

👻 Kyoto Ghost Tour — Bamboo Forest at Night

Arashiyama · ~$70 CAD per person · ~2 hours · Meets 7:30 PM · Ages 16+

After the family heads home, here's an option for your first night in Kyoto: a ghost tour through the Arashiyama Bamboo Forest after dark. The same bamboo grove you may have walked through during the day — but pitch black, empty, and full of ghost stories.

A guide leads a small group through the forest, sharing dark folklore, real historical tales, and local legends of ghostly samurai and vanished lovers. At one point, you walk through the bamboo completely alone. The reviews are overwhelmingly positive — people describe it as funny, genuinely creepy, and one of the most memorable things they did in Kyoto.

Details: Meet at Saga-Arashiyama Station (JR line, round window near the North Gate upstairs) at 7:30 PM. The tour runs about 2 hours. No recording or live streaming allowed. Not suitable for under 16, which is perfect since it's a D&C-only evening.

Tip: Don't visit the bamboo forest during the day if you're doing this tour — the guide specifically asks that you experience it fresh after dark. Since the family day doesn't include Arashiyama, you're all set.
