Welcome

Your home base is Osaka

You'll be flying with us from Vancouver and staying at our Airbnb in Nishikujo — a real neighbourhood a short walk from the train station. From here, all of Kansai is within reach: Kyoto 45 minutes, Kobe 25, Nara 30.

On guided days, we'll be your personal tour guides. On homeschool days (Tuesdays and Thursdays), you'll explore independently with self-guided itineraries. Your three weeks include a four-day bullet train journey to Mt. Fuji and Lake Hamana, a fairytale forest village, a hot spring ryokan, Universal Studios, and a birthday dinner you won't forget. You'll fly home with Andy on November 23rd.

Days 1–6

Arrive & Discover

You're flying with us from Vancouver, and the plan from the moment we land is to stay moving — the best way to beat jetlag. First evening out in Umeda for shopping and a sushi train welcome dinner. Saturday we'll do a walking tour of Osaka's wow highlights. After a rest day on Sunday, we all head to Kyoto together — the family day trips home, but you stay overnight so you get a full self-guided day to explore Kyoto on your own.

Sat Nov 7

Osaka Walking Tour

A guided walk through the wow highlights of Osaka — castle views, neon-lit streets, street food, and the energy of a city that never stops eating.

Mon Nov 9

Kyoto Day Tour + D&C Overnight

Kiyomizu-dera's wooden terrace, the squeaky nightingale floors of Nijo Castle, Gion's lantern-lit lanes, and maybe the Kyoto Railway Museum if the kids have legs left. The family heads home — you stay the night in Kyoto!

Kyoto on Your Own

Kids are in lessons back in Osaka, so this one's all yours. Arashiyama bamboo grove, Kinkaku-ji, Nishiki Market, and sake tasting in the Fushimi brewery district. We'll provide a walking route and restaurant picks.

See your itinerary →
Days 7–12

The Tokaido Shinkansen Crawl

The centrepiece of your trip — a four-day bullet train journey east along the historic Tokaido corridor. Mt. Fuji views from the foot of the mountain itself, a private visit to Tamiya Headquarters in Shizuoka, a fairytale forest village straight out of a Ghibli film, mandarin orange picking, fighter jets at an air force museum, and a hot spring ryokan with kaiseki dinner on the shores of Lake Hamana. Three cities, four days, all connected by Japan's most iconic train.

Thu Nov 12

Shin-Fuji & Shizuoka

Ekiben lunch on the shinkansen as the countryside unfolds. Arrive at the foot of Mt. Fuji for the World Heritage Centre and the ancient Sengen Shrine. Then a short Kodama hop to Shizuoka — Japan's model-making capital — for the night.

See day guide →
Fri Nov 13

Tamiya HQ, Fighter Jets & Gyoza

Dan & Connie start the morning at Tamiya Headquarters in Shizuoka — the archive room, showroom with full-size race cars, and the Tamiya shop. Meanwhile, the family shinkansens ahead to Hamamatsu for the JASDF Air Park (real fighter jets, cockpit simulators, free!) and Hamamatsu gyoza for lunch. Everyone meets up at Hamamatsu Station for a Costco run and hotel overnight.

See day guide →
Sat Nov 14

Nukumori no Mori & Onsen

Head to the Kanzanji area and step into a real-life Ghibli village — whimsical mushroom-shaped cottages, winding stone paths, gelato, and an owl café. Afternoon check-in at a Kanzanji Onsen ryokan on Lake Hamana. Hot springs and kaiseki dinner included.

See day guide →
Sun Nov 15

Orange Picking & Home

Before heading home, a morning of seasonal mandarin orange picking in the fields near Kanzanji — November is peak season and the kids will love it. Then shinkansen back to Osaka in time for a relaxed evening.

See day guide →
Days 13–19

Final Week Highlights

The CUPNOODLES Museum where you design your own cup, Universal Studios Japan with the whole crew, Sam's birthday dinner, Adrienne arriving from Tokyo, a day trip to the serene tea town of Uji, and Adrienne's birthday dinner to round it out. The pace eases off — these days are about enjoying each other's company before you blast off home with Andy.

Wed Nov 18

Nintendo World, Harry Potter, and all the rides. USJ is just a 10-minute train ride from our front door. Full day of fun for everyone.
