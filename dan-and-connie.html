Welcome

Your home base is Osaka

You'll be flying with us from Vancouver and staying at our Airbnb in Nishikujo — a real neighbourhood a short walk from the train station. From here, all of Kansai is within reach: Kyoto 45 minutes, Kobe 25, Nara 30.

On guided days, we'll be your personal tour guides. On homeschool days (Tuesdays and Thursdays), you'll explore independently with self-guided itineraries. Your three weeks include a bullet train ride, a Kobe beef dinner, Japan's most spectacular castle, a hot spring day trip, Universal Studios, and a birthday dinner you won't forget. Days 1–6

Arrive & Discover

You're flying with us from Vancouver, and the plan from the moment we land is to stay moving — the best way to beat jetlag. First evening out in Umeda for shopping and a sushi train welcome dinner. Saturday we'll do a walking tour of Osaka's wow highlights. After a rest day on Sunday, we all head to Kyoto together — the family day trips home, but you stay overnight so you get a full self-guided day to explore Kyoto on your own.

Sat Nov 7

Osaka Walking Tour

A guided walk through the wow highlights of Osaka — castle views, neon-lit streets, street food, and the energy of a city that never stops eating.

Mon Nov 9

Kyoto Day Tour + D&C Overnight

Fushimi Inari's thousand orange torii gates, Kiyomizu-dera temple, the charming lanes of Ninenzaka, and an evening in Gion. The family heads home — you stay the night in Kyoto!

Tue Nov 10 — Self-Guided

Kyoto on Your Own

Kids are in lessons back in Osaka, so this one's all yours. Arashiyama bamboo grove, Nishiki Market, more temples — explore at your own pace. We'll provide a walking route and restaurant picks.

Days 7–12

Kobe & Himeji

After a day of Osaka touring together, we head to Kobe — a stylish harbour city wedged between mountains and sea. You may head to a traditional ryokan in Arima Onsen for a private hot spring experience while the family stays in Kobe. Then we regroup for Chinatown, the waterfront, Kobe beef, and a morning at Himeji Castle — Japan's most spectacular original castle. A few more Kobe sights and we train home.

Thu Nov 12

Train to Kobe

Morning school/work, then we head to Kobe. You may head to a ryokan in Arima Onsen for a private couples' hot spring night while we check into the Kobe hotel.

Sat Nov 14

Himeji Castle

The White Heron Castle — UNESCO World Heritage Site, one of 12 original Japanese castles. Five stories, stunning views, and genuinely awe-inspiring architecture. Just 30 minutes from Kobe by train.

Fri Nov 13

Kobe Touring + Beef Dinner

Nankinmachi (Chinatown), Meriken Park waterfront, the harbour area. Cap it off with the real deal — authentic Kobe beef dinner.

Optional Alternate

Alternate Paths

We're keeping two possible swaps on the table. Instead of the Kobe & Himeji multi-day block, there's the option to journey to the iconic Mt. Fuji region — stay at a lakeside ryokan with private onsen, take in one of Japan's most legendary views, and return via the historic city of Kanazawa. And separately, on Day 14, the option to swap Universal Studios for a Kobe day trip instead.

Day 8 — Thu Nov 12

Travel to Kawaguchiko

Train from Osaka to the Fuji Five Lakes region. Check into a traditional ryokan on the shores of Lake Kawaguchi — hot spring baths with Mt. Fuji views.

Day 9 — Fri Nov 13

Lake Kawaguchi

A full day exploring the lake, mountain scenery, and surrounding trails. Late-autumn colours framing Japan's most iconic peak.

Day 10 — Sat Nov 14

Return via Kanazawa

Head west through Kanazawa — one of Japan's best-preserved castle towns. Kenroku-en Garden, the samurai district, and fresh seafood at Omi-cho Market before the train home.

Day 14 — Wed Nov 18

Kobe Day Trip

Instead of Universal Studios — a bullet train to Kobe for Nankinmachi Chinatown, the Meriken Park waterfront, and an authentic Kobe beef lunch. Back in Osaka by evening.

These are possible alternatives to the standard itinerary above. We'll make the final call together closer to the trip — no need to decide now.

Days 13–19

Final Week Highlights

The CUPNOODLES Museum where you design your own cup, Universal Studios Japan with the whole crew, Sam's birthday dinner, Adrienne arriving from Tokyo, a day trip to the serene tea town of Uji, and Adrienne's birthday dinner to round it out. The pace eases off — these days are about enjoying each other's company before you blast off home with Andy.

Wed Nov 18

Universal Studios Japan

Nintendo World, Harry Potter, and all the rides. USJ is just a 10-minute train ride from our front door. Full day of fun for everyone.

Tue Nov 17

CUPNOODLES Museum

The birthplace of instant ramen! Head to Ikeda (30 min train) to visit the original museum where Momofuku Ando invented Cup Noodle. Design your own custom cup, pick your toppings, and take it home.

Sat Nov 21

Uji & Adrienne's Birthday

A peaceful tea town with Byodo-in Temple (it's on the 10-yen coin!), matcha ice cream, and a river walk. Back to Osaka for Adrienne's birthday dinner.
