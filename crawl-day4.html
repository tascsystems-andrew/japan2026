Day 4 — Final Day

A Sweet Ending

The last morning of the crawl is a gentle one. After breakfast at the ryokan, head to the nearby orange farms for some mandarin picking, then make your way back to Hamamatsu Station and catch the shinkansen home to Osaka. You'll be back in time for a relaxed evening at the Airbnb.

8:00

Ryokan Breakfast

Sansuikan Kinryu

Traditional Japanese breakfast — grilled fish, rice, miso soup, pickles, tamagoyaki, nori. It's served in the same style as last night's kaiseki — lots of small, beautiful dishes. Fuel up before the last adventure.

🧒 Kids love: Rice and miso soup are kid staples. The grilled fish might be a harder sell, but there's usually toast and juice as backup at most ryokans.
9:30

Check Out + Luggage

Sansuikan Kinryu

Check out and ask the ryokan if they can hold luggage or arrange a taxi. The orange farms are nearby — about 10 minutes from the Kanzanji area. Flat, easy terrain perfect for little legs.

🚕 Short taxi to the orange farms (~10 min from Kanzanji)
10:00

Mandarin Orange Picking

Mikkabi / Kanzanji area · Typically ¥800–1,200/person · ~1 hour

November is peak mikan (mandarin orange) season around Lake Hamana, and the Mikkabi area is one of Japan's most famous growing regions. The farms have flat, easy fields — no hillside climbing — and you pick straight from the trees. Most farms let you eat as many as you want on-site and take a bag home.

The oranges in November are perfectly sweet and thin-skinned — nothing like what you get at the supermarket back home. The kids will eat their weight in them.

🧒 Kids love: Picking fruit off real trees (simple as that), eating as many as they want (they'll turn orange), and the flat, safe fields that are easy to run around in.

Tip: Bring a bag or buy one there — you'll want to take some back to Osaka. They also make great gifts.

🚕 Taxi back to Hamamatsu Station (~30–40 min from Kanzanji area). Collect luggage if stored at ryokan, or bring it with you.
12:30

Hamamatsu Station

Pick up ekiben for the ride home

Grab lunch for the train — another round of ekiben from the station, or pick up something from the shops. The shinkansen home is about 1.5 hours.

Tip: Hamamatsu Station has a good selection of local ekiben. Look for the unagi (eel) bento — Hamamatsu is famous for its eel, and the train bento version is excellent.

🚅 Green Car shinkansen Hamamatsu → Shin-Osaka (~1.5 hrs on Hikari)
14:30

Home — Osaka Airbnb

Nishikujo

Back to base. Unpack the Costco haul, unload the oranges, put the kids down, and enjoy a quiet evening at the Airbnb. Tomorrow is a rest day — you've earned it.
