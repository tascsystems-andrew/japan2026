Last Morning in Kyoto

You've had your Big Bird moment. Today is about soaking in one more piece of Kyoto before heading back to Osaka and your family. Nishiki Market is sensory magic for kids — pickles, mochi, grilled seafood, and matcha everything. If everyone has energy, Fushimi Inari's thousands of vermillion torii gates are worth a quick visit. Then it's back to Osaka for a relaxed evening.

The day: Check out of your hotel this morning, do Nishiki Market, grab lunch near Kyoto Station, and head home on the train. The afternoon is yours to unwind with the grandkids.
Sunday, December 13

8:00

Breakfast at Richmond Hotel + Check Out

Final morning at the hotel

One last excellent hotel breakfast. Fuel up well — you've got exploring to do and a train ride ahead. Check out is 11 AM. Pack your bags, leave them with the hotel if you want a few hours of wandering before heading to the station.

9:00

Nishiki Market — "Kyoto's Kitchen"

Nakagyo · Free entry · ~10 min walk from hotel

A five-block covered market packed with hundreds of stalls selling pickles, mochi, fresh seafood, grilled food on sticks, matcha sweets, and every kind of Japanese delicacy you can imagine. This is lunch, snack time, and pure sensory entertainment all in one. Walk slowly, let the kids taste things, grab omiyage (souvenirs) for people back home.

The market is relatively quiet on Sunday mornings before the tourist crush, so you'll get a genuine glimpse of how locals shop and eat.

🧒 Kids experience: The market is a treasure hunt. Each stall is a new discovery — colours, smells, little food samples. The octopus-on-a-stick, the colourful mochi, the mound of pickles. Even the youngest kids will be amazed.

Tip: Give each kid a small budget (¥500–1000) to pick their own snack. They'll feel like grown-up explorers, and you'll get photos of their genuine delight.

10:30

Optional — Fushimi Inari Taisha (Quick Visit)

Fushimi · Free · ~10 min train from Kyoto Station

If you didn't get here yesterday and the group still has energy, Fushimi Inari is worth a side trip. The thousands of vermillion torii gates winding up the mountainside are iconic and magical. You don't need to go to the top (2+ hours) — even 15–20 minutes walking up through the gate tunnels will give you the iconic photos and the sense of the place.

Train from Kyoto Station to Fushimi Inari is quick and easy. Come back to Kyoto Station after for lunch and the ride home.

🧒 Kids love: Running through the endless tunnel of orange gates feels like stepping into another world. It's magical and immediately impressive, even to little kids.

Tip: This is optional. If you're content with Nishiki Market and want to just grab lunch and head home, that's perfectly fine. You've already had an extraordinary trip.

11:30

Lunch Near Kyoto Station

Grab something good

You're near Kyoto Station anyway, heading for the train. There are hundreds of food options here — the massive underground food hall, ramen shops, bento restaurants, conveyor belt sushi, anything you want. Find a spot, sit down, eat well, and enjoy a moment of rest before the train ride home.

🚃 JR Special Rapid from Kyoto → Osaka (~30 min). Settle in, maybe let the kids rest or play a quiet game. You're heading home.
14:00

Arrive Back in Osaka

Return to the Airbnb

You're back. The Kyoto chapter is closed. Get to the Airbnb, drop your bags, and settle back into family time. The grandkids have been waiting — dinner together, stories about Kyoto, showing them what you brought back from Nishiki Market. This is home base.

Evening

Relaxed Evening at the Airbnb

Family time

You've earned a quiet night. Maybe a walk around the neighbourhood if everyone has energy. Maybe dinner at home or a local restaurant. Mostly, just be present with your family. The big adventure is done. Now it's about being together.

🧒 Grandkid perspective: They've had you to themselves for a day — tell them about the bamboo forest, the golden statues, the market. They want to know what you saw and felt. Share the stories.

🏨 Check Out — Richmond Hotel Premier Kyoto Shijo

Check-out time: 11 AM. If you want to explore a bit longer, you can usually leave luggage with the hotel and pick it up before heading to the station.

On your way home: Collect any luggage you've left behind, grab any last snacks or supplies from the LIFE supermarket downstairs, and head to Kyoto Station for your train home.
