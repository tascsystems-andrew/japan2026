The Plan

Whisky While They Do Noodles

While the family heads to the CUPNOODLES Museum in Ikeda, you two get a morning at Suntory's Yamazaki Distillery — where Japanese whisky was born in 1923. It's a short train ride from Osaka, sitting right on the Kyoto-Osaka border in a misty valley where three rivers converge.

🎰 The Lottery: The full MONOZUKURI distillery tour (the one with the fermentation rooms, cask warehouse, and tasting session) is allocated by lottery only. Entries for November open around early September 2026 on suntory.com. We'll enter as soon as it opens. If we don't win, the Whisky Museum is free and excellent on its own — see Scenario B below.
Two Ways This Day Goes
Scenario A — We win the lottery

Full MONOZUKURI Distillery Tour + Tasting

~80 minutes guided through the mashing room, fermentation washbacks, copper pot stills, and cask warehouse. The air changes from sake-like to whisky as you go. Ends with a curated tasting of exclusive Yamazaki expressions and a lesson in highball-making. Audio guide in English available.

Afterwards, you'll have free time in the Whisky Museum + shop.

Scenario B — We don't win (still great)

Yamazaki Whisky Museum (Free, Walk-In)

The museum is free and open to walk-in visitors — just register at the entrance on arrival. Two floors covering the history of Japanese whisky, Suntory's story, and the distillation process with miniature models and English descriptions.

The ground floor has the Whisky Library — a stunning wall of hundreds of bottles in every shade of amber — and a pay-per-dram tasting counter (¥300–800 per pour). You can try distillery-only expressions you won't find anywhere else. The gift shop has exclusive small-batch bottles and Yamazaki merch.

9:00

Depart Osaka

JR Osaka Station → JR Yamazaki Station

Take the JR Kyoto Line (regular train, not shinkansen) from Osaka Station. It's about 25 minutes to Yamazaki Station. The distillery is a 10-minute walk from the station through a quiet residential neighbourhood.

Tip: Yamazaki Station is tiny — one platform, one exit. You can't miss it.

Suntory Yamazaki Distillery

Shimamoto · Tour: lottery / Museum: free · 9:30–17:00

If Scenario A: Your tour will be at a reserved time (likely 10:00 or 10:30). ~80 min guided tour through the full production line, ending with tasting. Then free time in museum + shop.

If Scenario B: Head straight to the Whisky Museum. Take your time through the exhibits, then settle in at the tasting counter. Three pours each from the distillery-exclusive menu is a good pace. The whisky library photo wall alone is worth the visit.

Tip: The gift shop has distillery-only bottles and small tasting sets that make incredible souvenirs. Go to the shop first — popular items sell out by afternoon.
