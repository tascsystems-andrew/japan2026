The Trip

Two adventures in one

First: four days solo in Tokyo — one of the most electrifying cities on earth, entirely at your pace. Spa days, street food, tiny bars, and world-class art.

Then: the bullet train to Osaka to join the family for Sam's birthday, ten days of Kansai life, and a girls' overnight in Kyoto with Sam and the kids. Oh — and we'll be celebrating your birthday too.

You've traveled enough to know that the best trips are the ones where you let the place surprise you. Japan will do that every single day.

Part One

Tokyo Solo

These are suggestions, not a schedule. Think of them as a menu to pick from — wander between them, skip what doesn't call to you, and let yourself get lost. The best moments in Tokyo are the ones you don't plan.

Sat Nov 15 — Arrival

Shibuya at Night

The world's most famous intersection, lit up in neon. A tiny yakitori bar in Nonbei Yokocho makes a perfect first-night dinner.

Mon Nov 16 — Suggestion

Harajuku & Meiji Shrine

Takeshita Street chaos meets Omotesando's architectural calm. Meiji Shrine is a massive forest in the middle of the city. Consider Shibuya Sky at sunset for panoramic views.

Tue Nov 17 — Don't miss this one

Senso-ji & teamLab Borderless

Tokyo's oldest temple is magical early morning before the crowds. And teamLab Borderless is the one thing everyone says you have to do — immersive digital art that feels like walking inside a dream. Book tickets at teamlab.art well in advance.

Wed Nov 18 — Treat Yourself

Spa Day & Golden Gai

Thermae-Yu in Shinjuku is a multi-floor spa/onsen perfect for a solo morning. For your last night, Golden Gai's six tiny alleys of 4-seat bars are solo traveler paradise.

Thu Nov 19 — Bullet Train Day

Shinkansen to Osaka

Grab an ekiben bento at Tokyo Station, board the shinkansen, and watch for Mt. Fuji out the right-side window. Arrive in time for Sam's birthday dinner.

Part Two

Two Birthdays in Japan

Thursday evening, November 19. You arrive from Tokyo just in time for Sam's birthday — Kobe beef teppanyaki dinner, cooked right in front of you. All nine of us around the grill.

Two days later, it's your turn. Saturday the 21st — a day trip to the tea town of Uji, then back to Osaka for a combined farewell dinner for Dan & Connie and a birthday celebration for you.

Welcome to the family.

Part Three

Osaka & Family Time

The pace shifts. Four kids under 7 set the tempo. It's a different kind of travel — but you'll see Japan through their eyes, and that has its own magic.

Fri Nov 20

Osaka Walkabout

First full day in Osaka. No big agenda — Dotonbori, Shinsaibashi, or just the neighbourhood. Dan & Connie are still here. Enjoy the overlap.

Sat Nov 21

Uji Day Trip + YOUR Birthday Dinner!

A peaceful tea town 30 minutes away. Byodo-in Temple (it's on the 10-yen coin), matcha ice cream, river walk. That evening: a combined farewell dinner for Dan & Connie and birthday celebration for you. Happy birthday in Japan!

Sun Nov 22

Rest Day

Dan & Connie depart. Quiet day at the Airbnb. Recharge.

Mon Nov 23

Andy Departs for Vancouver

Andy flies back for his work week. It's you, Sam, and four kids. You've got this.

The Highlight

Girls' Trip to Kyoto

Tuesday after homeschool, you and Sam pack up the kids and take the train to Kyoto for two nights. Apartment hotel with a kitchen. Nishiki Market for lunch. Kamo River for the kids to run.

Wednesday is a full free day — Higashiyama temples, Arashiyama bamboo grove, or just wandering Gion. No pressure. The accomplishment is the trip itself.

Thursday morning, pack up, train home. You just navigated Kyoto with four kids in a foreign country. Not bad.

What to Wear

Layers. 15–18°C days, 7–10°C evenings. Comfortable walking shoes — 15,000+ steps/day in Tokyo. Slip-ons for temples.

Money & Phone

Japan is cash-heavy. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards. Get an eSIM before you leave. Google Maps is essential.

Book Now

teamLab Borderless tickets at teamlab.art (they sell out!). Tokyo hotel in Shibuya or Shinjuku.

Golden Gai

Some bars have cover charges (¥500–1,000), posted on the door. Totally normal. One drink per bar, hop to the next.

The Shinkansen

Reserved seats recommended. Green Car is business class if you want to treat yourself. Ekiben bento boxes at the station are the move.

For Kyoto

Pack light for 2 nights. Carrier or stroller for the kids (not both). Konbini everywhere for emergency supplies.
