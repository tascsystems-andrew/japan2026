Day 2 — Split Morning

Two Adventures, One City

Today the group splits up for the morning. Dan & Connie stay in Shizuoka for a visit to Tamiya Headquarters. The family shinkansens ahead to Hamamatsu for fighter jets and gyoza. Everyone meets back up at Hamamatsu Station in the afternoon for a Costco run and hotel check-in.

The Morning Split
Dan & Connie

Tamiya Headquarters — Shizuoka

A short taxi from the hotel (~10 min). The archive room takes you through Tamiya's history from wooden models to modern RC cars. The showroom lobby has full-size race cars, the Monster Beetle, and F1 cars. The Tamiya shop has discounted kits and exclusive merch. Free admission, reservation required (submitted in advance).

Hours: Mon–Fri, 9:00–16:30. Max group: 5 people.

After the visit, shinkansen from Shizuoka to Hamamatsu (~25 min) to meet the family.

Family

JASDF Air Park — Hamamatsu

Shinkansen from Shizuoka to Hamamatsu (~25 min), then taxi to the Air Park (~15 min). This is the Japan Air Self-Defense Force museum — real fighter jets you can get up close to, cockpit simulators, flight suits, and aviation history. Free admission.

After the museum, Hamamatsu gyoza for lunch — the city's signature dish. Head back to Hamamatsu Station to meet D&C.

🧒 Kids love: Climbing near real fighter jets, the cockpit simulators, and pretending to be pilots. Free admission makes it even better.

🚕 Taxi to Costco Hamamatsu (~15 min from station)
14:30

Costco Hamamatsu

Hamakita Ward · Costco hours

Stock up on snacks, supplies, and anything you've been missing from home. Costco Japan has all the familiar stuff plus Japanese exclusives — matcha Kit Kats in bulk, Japanese whisky at good prices, and excellent fresh sushi platters.

🧒 Kids love: The food court. Same hot dogs, same pizza, same soft serve. A taste of home in the middle of Japan.

Tip: Bring your Costco membership card from home — it works worldwide.

🚕 Taxi to hotel (~15 min back to station area)
17:00

Check In — Richmond Hotel Hamamatsu

3 rooms · Near Hamamatsu Station

Drop the bags, settle in. If you're not Costco'd out for dinner, Hamamatsu gyoza is the move — the city is famous for it. Look for a gyoza shop near the station (they serve them in a distinctive circular pattern). Or just enjoy whatever you picked up at Costco.

Tip: If D&C missed gyoza at lunch, tonight's the night. Hamamatsu-style gyoza is pan-fried in a circle and served with bean sprouts. It's different from Tokyo or Osaka gyoza.
