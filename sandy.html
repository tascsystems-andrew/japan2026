The Main Event

Kyoto Overnight

An overnight trip to Japan's ancient capital — temples, bamboo forests, market streets, and the places Big Bird walked in 1988. Kyoto is just 30 minutes from Osaka by train, but staying the night means you can take your time, linger over dinner, and wake up in one of the most beautiful cities in the world.

Sun Dec 13 — Morning

Sanjusangendo Temple

Japan's longest wooden building, housing 1,001 golden statues of Kannon, the goddess of mercy — standing in ten rows, stretching into the distance. This is where Barkley got spooked and ran away in Big Bird in Japan. No photos allowed inside, but you'll remember it forever. ¥600 adults, ¥300 kids.

Sun Dec 13 — Afternoon

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

The towering bamboo forest on the western edge of Kyoto — the same forest that inspired the Tale of the Bamboo Cutter, the fairy tale at the heart of Big Bird in Japan. In December the crowds thin out, and the light filtering through the bamboo is magical. Free and open all day.

Sun Dec 13 — Evening

Kyoto by Night

Check in to the hotel, freshen up, then a walk through Gion or Pontocho for dinner. December is peak autumn in Kyoto — temples and gardens may still be lit up with fall illuminations. End the night with a warm bowl of udon.

Good to Know

Flights: You'll need to book your own flights to Kansai International Airport (KIX). Direct options from Vancouver are limited — most routes connect through Tokyo (NRT/HND), Seoul (ICN), or Taipei (TPE).

Weather: Mid-December in Osaka/Kyoto averages 5–10°C (40–50°F). Pack layers and a warm coat. The kids will be in full winter mode by then.
