Osaka → Kyoto · Temples · Bamboo Grove
8:00

Early Breakfast at the Airbnb

Before heading out

Eat well before you go. You've got a full day ahead and the grandkids will do better with food in them. Pack snacks (fruit, crackers, juice boxes) for between stops — Kyoto temples and trains can work up an appetite, and little legs tire faster than you'd think.

🚃 JR Special Rapid from Osaka → Kyoto (~30 min). Get seats together if possible, especially since you have the little ones.
9:30

Sanjusangendo Temple — The Big Bird Stop

Higashiyama · ¥600 adults, ¥300 children · ~45 min visit

This is where Barkley got spooked in the movie. Sanjusangendo is a long wooden hall housing 1,001 golden Kannon statues — the atmosphere is hushed, golden, and genuinely awe-inspiring. The kids will feel it. Photography is not allowed inside, so you're all going to experience it together, not through a phone screen.

The approach to the temple is beautiful, and the neighbourhood is quiet and traditional. Plan about 45 minutes for the temple visit itself.

🧒 Kids experience: The sight of 1,001 golden statues is stunning at any age. The little ones might be a bit overwhelmed by the quiet reverence, but the older kids (6 and 7) will understand they're seeing something special. It's a moment.

Tip: No photos inside means everyone is present. That's actually a gift.

11:00

Kiyomizu-dera Temple

Higashiyama · ¥400 · Famous wooden terrace

The famous wooden terrace juts out over the hillside, giving you views across all of Kyoto. The approach streets (Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka) are lined with traditional shops and snack vendors — souvenir shops, matcha ice cream stands, little restaurants. December may still have some late autumn colours, and the whole area is beautiful for wandering.

Let the kids lead you through the approach streets. They'll find the snacks, the shops, the small discoveries that make Japan magical for children.

🧒 Kids love: The approach streets are basically a treasure hunt — matcha ice cream, little toys in shop windows, the general energy of a famous spot. The terrace itself is a thrill (they're up high!), and the view is impressive to even the youngest kid.
12:30

Lunch in Higashiyama

Plenty of options nearby

You're in the heart of Higashiyama, surrounded by restaurants and food stalls. Grab udon, soba, tempura, or whatever looks good. The streets around Kiyomizu are designed for wandering and eating. Plenty of places are kid-friendly, and the whole vibe is casual.

Tip: Early lunch (before 1 PM) means shorter waits and happier kids. Pick something that looks good to you — you can't go wrong.

🚃 Train/bus to Arashiyama (west Kyoto, ~30 min from Higashiyama). Bus is more scenic, train is faster.
14:30

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — The Fairy Tale Forest

Ukyo · Free · Open all day

This is the forest from the Big Bird movie — the magical bamboo grove from the Tale of the Bamboo Cutter / Kaguya-hime (the Japanese Princess story). The bamboo towers above you, the light filters through in shafts, and the air feels different. It's genuinely enchanting.

The path through is easy and flat — perfect for all ages. Walk slowly, let the kids lead you, sit on a bench if the moment feels right. This is the heart of your trip.

🧒 Kids experience: Even the youngest will understand they're in something magical. The bamboo is alive and creaking, the light is beautiful, and it feels like stepping into a story. The older kids (6 and 7) will be absolutely enchanted.
Open in Maps
15:30

Togetsukyo Bridge & Arashiyama Main Street

Free · Walking area

Leave the bamboo grove and walk to the iconic Togetsukyo Bridge — wide, dramatic, and instantly recognizable. The main street around the bridge is lined with souvenir shops, restaurants, and food stalls. Grab a snack, let the kids browse the shop windows, enjoy the energy of a famous spot without any rushing.

If energy permits, Tenryu-ji Temple is nearby (¥500 admission) with a beautiful garden — but it's optional. The bridge and the street alone are plenty for one afternoon.

🚃 Train/walk to hotel area (Shijo). ~30 min depending on your route. Take it easy — everyone's had a full day.
17:00

Check In to Richmond Hotel Premier Kyoto Shijo

Shijo-dori · Central Kyoto

Drop your bags, get settled, and take a breath. You made it through the full Big Bird tour. The kids are tired, you're probably tired too, and a good rest is coming. The hotel lounge (free drinks and snacks until 9 PM) is perfect if anyone needs a quiet decompression moment.

18:30

Dinner Walk — Pontocho or Gion

Shijo area · Walking distance from hotel

You're right in the centre of Kyoto's best neighbourhoods. Pontocho is a narrow pedestrian alley along the river, lantern-lit and atmospheric — restaurants ranging from casual to fancy, all with views of the water. Gion (the geisha district) is nearby and beautiful at night. Choose whatever feels right for your group's energy level.

December illuminations may still be happening at some temples if you want to extend the evening, but a good dinner and an early night is also perfectly wise.

Tip: The kids have had an extraordinary day. A nice dinner and bed is the right call. You've already given them the memory of a lifetime.
