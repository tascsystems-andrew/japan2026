Day 1

The Crawl Begins

The shinkansen crawl kicks off with a bullet train ride east along the Tokaido corridor. Ekiben bento boxes on the train, Mt. Fuji up close in the afternoon, then a short hop to Shizuoka for the night.

Seating tip: On the shinkansen heading east from Shin-Osaka, sit on the right side (seats D and E) for the best Mt. Fuji views. The mountain appears about 90–100 minutes into the ride, near Shizuoka.
10:00

Shin-Osaka Station — All Aboard!

Green Car · Kodama or Hikari

Morning homeschool and work done, then everyone heads to Shin-Osaka Station. Before boarding, grab an ekiben (train bento) from the station — there are entire shops dedicated to them. Each one is a little work of art. Pick a couple that look good and settle in for the ride.

🧒 Kids love: Choosing their own ekiben box (the ones shaped like trains are always a hit), watching the countryside fly past, and the excitement of finally being ON the bullet train.

Tip: The ekiben shops are inside the shinkansen gates. Look for "Ekiben" or "駅弁" signs. Budget ¥1,000–1,500 per box.

🚅 Green Car shinkansen Shin-Osaka → Shin-Fuji (~2.5 hrs on Kodama). Lunch on the train!
13:00

Mt. Fuji World Heritage Centre

Fujinomiya · ¥300 adults, free under 6 · 9:00–17:00

A striking inverted-cone building with a spiral ramp that takes you on a virtual climb of Mt. Fuji — projections on the walls shift through the seasons as you ascend. At the top, if the weather cooperates, real Fuji is framed in the observation window. It's a beautiful museum that works for all ages.

🧒 Kids love: The spiral ramp "climb" (feels like an adventure), the virtual reality Fuji summit experience, and the giant Fuji photo opportunity at the top.

🚕 Taxi ~10 min to Sengen Shrine (or walk ~20 min if the weather is nice)
14:30

Fujisan Hongu Sengen Taisha

Fujinomiya · Free · Grounds open dawn to dusk

The most sacred shrine on Mt. Fuji — the starting point for pilgrims climbing the mountain for centuries. The grounds are ancient and peaceful, with towering cedar trees and a stunning spring-fed pond (Wakutama-ike) where the snowmelt from Fuji's summit emerges crystal clear. Kids can splash near the water's edge.

🧒 Kids love: The crystal-clear sacred pond, the massive old trees, and the sense of being somewhere really special and really old.

Tip: Try Fujinomiya yakisoba (local fried noodles) from a street stall near the shrine — thick chewy noodles with a rich sauce. It's a local specialty.

🚕 Taxi back to Shin-Fuji Station (~15 min). Then 🚅 Kodama to Shizuoka (1 stop, ~15 min).
17:00

Check In — Shizuoka

Kuretake Inn Premium Shizuokaekimae · 3 rooms

A short Kodama hop from Shin-Fuji to Shizuoka — Japan's model-making capital and home to Tamiya (which Dan & Connie will visit tomorrow morning). The Kuretake Inn is right by the station. Shizuoka is a lively city with great food — look for shirasu (whitebait) dishes and Shizuoka oden from street stalls.

Tip: Kuretake Inns often have a complimentary evening drink service and free breakfast. Check at reception.
