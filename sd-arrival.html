Arrival · Settle In · First Hugs
Midday

Land at Kansai International Airport (KIX)

Southern Osaka · Welcome to Japan

You've made it. Collect your luggage, clear immigration and customs (be prepared for a small form), and head out to ground transportation. The airport is modern, clean, and relatively easy to navigate. Follow signs for "Trains" or "Rental Cars."

Tip: Your phone likely won't have service yet. Grab a SIM card or pocket WiFi from the airport shops before you leave if you haven't arranged one already.

PM

KIX → Airbnb (Choose Your Route)

~70 minutes

Option 1 — Haruka Express: The Haruka Limited Express train is fast and straightforward. It leaves from KIX directly to Kyoto Station and other points — ask at the station if it goes toward Osaka/Nishikujo. Cost is around ¥3,000–4,000. Takes luggage easily and feels smooth after a long flight.

Option 2 — Rental Car: If your son is picking you up or if you've arranged a car, this is the easiest option for luggage and settling in at your own pace. The drive from KIX to the Kasugadekita address is about 60–70 minutes depending on traffic.

Option 3 — Taxi: Comfortable but pricey (around ¥10,000–12,000).

Tip: The Haruka is convenient, but if you're tired and have a lot of luggage, the rental car or being picked up is worth it. This is not the day to figure out train schedules.

16:00

Arrive at the Airbnb · Settle In

Kasugadekita, Konohana Ward

You're here. Get your luggage in the door, do a quick orientation with your son, and let the grandkids show you around. They've been waiting for this. There's no agenda — let this time happen naturally. Help them show you their favourite things in the house, let them sit with you, settle your nerves from the flight.

🧒 Grandkid energy: The 7-year-old and 6-year-old will want to show you everything. The younger two (4 and 3) might be shy at first but will warm up quickly once they see you're here to stay for five days. Give them a little space but let them lead.
17:00

Neighbourhood Walk · Keep It Low Key

Kasugadekita & Nishikujo · Walking distance

Once everyone's had a moment, take a gentle walk around the neighbourhood with whoever wants to go (maybe your son and one or two of the kids). The Konohana area has a charming local shotengai (shopping street) with small shops, fruit stands, and the energy of everyday Japan. Peek into a konbini (convenience store) — these are an experience in themselves.

You might also walk along the small canal near the Airbnb. Nothing formal, just a slow stroll to get your legs moving, see where things are, and help the kids burn a little energy before dinner.

🧒 Kids love: Konbinis. They're fascinating to children — all the snacks, the drinks, the novelty toys. Let them each pick a small snack or drink. It becomes their "welcome home" memory.

Tip: You're jet-lagged. Don't push it. A 20–30 minute walk is plenty. You've just flown across the world.

18:00

Optional — Osaka Castle Park at Dusk

If energy permits · JR from Nishikujo to Osakajo-Koen (~15 min)

If the group is still awake and interested, a quick evening visit to Osaka Castle Park is magical. In December, you'll catch the castle lit up beautifully at dusk — golden lights against the dark sky. The park itself is beautiful and peaceful in the evening, and it's a nice introduction to Osaka's landmarks without being overwhelming.

This is optional. If everyone is ready to head home and rest, skip it. There's no shame in a travel day that ends early.

19:00

Welcome Dinner at the Airbnb

Home-cooked meal with the family

Your son will likely have arranged a homecooked meal — his way of saying welcome home without making you leave the house. Eat together, let the kids see you're staying, enjoy being around everyone. This is the whole reason you came.

Bedtime will come early for everyone, and that's fine. You've all earned rest.

🏠 Your Home — Kasugadekita Airbnb

Location: Kasugadekita 2-chōme-9-24, Konohana Ward, Osaka 554-0021. Nearest station: Nishikujo (one JR stop from Universal Studios Japan).

Neighbourhood: Quiet residential area with local shops, restaurants, and the charm of everyday Osaka. Nothing touristy — just normal life.

Getting around: Nishikujo is one stop away on the JR Yume-Yuuki Line. From there, you can reach central Osaka, Kyoto, and other areas easily.

Shopping: Shotengai (shopping street) and konbinis nearby. The neighbourhood has everything you need for a comfortable stay.
