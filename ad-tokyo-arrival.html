First Night in Tokyo

You've arrived. The neon glow, the energy, the endless possibilities. Welcome to Tokyo.

Sunday, November 15, 2026

Your Narita Express Ticket

Your Narita Express ticket is included in your package. It's a reserved seat — find yours and settle in. The ride is smooth, quiet, and scenic. You'll go from rice paddies to suburbs to Tokyo's glittering towers in 90 minutes. Charge your phone, enjoy the WiFi, and let the anticipation build.

Midday

Land at Narita

Immigration, luggage, SIM card or pocket WiFi

You've landed. Take a breath. Clear immigration, collect your luggage, and head to the terminal shops. Grab a SIM card or pocket WiFi (both available at the airport). Then find your Narita Express platform.

14:00

Narita Express to Shinjuku

~90 minutes, reserved seat, included

Board the Narita Express with your pre-reserved seat. Watch the landscape transform: rice paddies give way to suburbs, then Tokyo's neon towers pierce the sky. The ride is smooth and quiet. There's WiFi and power outlets. Use this time to decompress, check your phone, or simply watch the city reveal itself.

Tip: Get a drink at a station shop before boarding — it'll be the cheapest beverage you'll get in central Tokyo.

16:00

Shinjuku Hotel Check-in

Freshen up, rest, recover

You've arrived at your hotel in Shinjuku. Check in, take a shower, and take a nap if you need it. Jet lag is real, and you have four full days ahead. There's no prize for pushing through exhaustion on your first night. Rest is part of the trip.

18:00

Your First Evening

Two options: choose your own adventure

Option A: Shibuya Crossing & Nonbei Yokocho

Take the train from Shinjuku to Shibuya (about 5 minutes). Stand above the Starbucks overlooking the world's most famous intersection and watch the choreography of 2,500 people crossing at once. Then cross it yourself — you're part of the show now.

For dinner, hunt down Nonbei Yokocho, a tiny alley of bars near Shibuya Station with yakitori joints seating 6-8 people. Sit at a counter, order skewers, and soak in the atmosphere. This is Tokyo's soul in miniature.

Tip: Most places only have Japanese menus, but the pictures are clear — point and smile.

Option B: Omoide Yokocho

Don't even leave Shinjuku. Walk 2 minutes from the station to Omoide Yokocho ("Memory Lane"). It's a narrow, atmospheric alley lined with tiny grilled-skewer stalls — exactly what it sounds like. Smoky, loud, tight, and completely magical at night.

Grab a stool at a yakitori counter. Order a beer. Watch the salarymen and travellers and locals. The bartenders are used to solo diners. This is where you become part of Tokyo on your first night.

Tip: It's smaller and less touristy than similar alleys. Much more authentic.

Late

Optional: Shibuya Sky or Just Wander

Tokyo doesn't sleep

If you have energy left, Shibuya Sky (¥2,200, open until 10pm) is a 360° open-air observation deck offering panoramic city views. The neon, the lights, Tokyo unfolds beneath you.

Or just walk around Shinjuku — Kabukicho, the pachinko parlours, the neon — and soak it in. There's no wrong choice here. Your body is running on adrenaline, and Tokyo is electric.

Solo tip: Your first night in Tokyo should feel electric. Pick ONE thing and do it well. Don't try to see everything tonight. You have three more days.
